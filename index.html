Project Abstract

Research Overview

SafeNethra is designed as a multilingual cyberbullying content detector extension supported by a centralized backend service. Once a user signs in to a connected social profile or page, the system monitors profile content, comments, image-based posts, and identity-related signals in real time. The platform aims to detect harmful Sinhala and English content, protect sensitive personal data, flag suspicious fake profiles, and generate structured reports for informed action.

Text Analysis

Detects abusive and bullying language from Sinhala and English textual interactions.

Image Moderation

Identifies cyberbullying signals embedded within shared images and visual media.

Sensitive Data Alerts

Highlights exposure of private details in harmful text or image-based content.

Fake Profile Identification

Supports recognition of suspicious accounts that may contribute to online harassment.

Core Functional Vision

Built for academic impact and practical safety

The extension-side experience focuses on user-facing monitoring and instant notifications, while the centralized backend handles deeper analysis, profile-level processing, and report generation. This dual-architecture makes the project suitable for both research evaluation and future real-world adaptation.
