The content below is arranged according to common academic website guidance: literature survey, research gap, problem, objectives, methodology, and technologies used.

Current cyberbullying research highlights the value of natural language processing, computer vision, and user behavior analysis. However, many available systems focus on English-only datasets, isolated content types, or offline batch analysis. SafeNetra extends this discussion by addressing bilingual moderation, multimodal detection, and live profile monitoring in a single research context.

Existing solutions often lack Sinhala language support, real-time analysis for social interactions, integrated image-based bullying detection, and mechanisms to identify privacy-sensitive content or fake profiles together. There is also limited emphasis on combining a user-side extension with a centralized analytical backend that can produce structured reports.

How can a research system accurately detect multilingual cyberbullying in both text and images, identify sensitive personal information exposure, recognize fake profiles, and support real-time monitoring of social media pages or profiles through a practical extension-plus-backend architecture?

  • Detect text-based cyberbullying content in Sinhala and English.
  • Detect image-based cyberbullying signals using vision-oriented analysis.
  • Identify sensitive personal data exposed in harmful text or imagery.
  • Recognize suspicious or fake profiles linked to harmful online behavior.
  • Generate reports and notifications through a centralized system.

The proposed workflow begins with authenticated extension access to a social profile or page. The system captures relevant profile data, comments, and images and securely passes them to a centralized backend. Text classification, OCR-assisted or image-based interpretation, privacy-sensitive pattern detection, and profile authenticity checks are then performed. Finally, the platform generates risk alerts and report summaries for review.
